DE VITA CAESARUM

The Twelve Caesars

"For my part, I think that the most beautiful thing in the world is the history of Rome." — C. Suetonius Tranquillus

I

Julius

100–44 BC

"Take we the course ... The die is cast."

II

Augustus

63 BC – AD 14

"He had found it built of brick and left it in marble."

III

Tiberius

42 BC – AD 37

"Alas for the Roman people, to be ground by jaws that crunch so slowly!"

IV

Caligula

AD 12–41

"I wish the Roman people had but a single neck."

V

Claudius

10 BC – AD 54

"He stole away ... and hid among the curtains which hung before the door."

VI

Nero

AD 37–68

"What an artist the world is losing!"

VII

Galba

3 BC – AD 69

"What mean you, fellow soldiers? I am yours and you are mine."

VIII

Otho

AD 32–69

"Let us add this one more night to our life."

IX

Vitellius

AD 15–69

"Be of good cheer; to us light is given."

X

Vespasian

AD 9–79

"Woe's me. Methinks I'm turning into a god."

XI

Titus

AD 39–81

"Friends, I have lost a day."

XII

Domitian

AD 51–96

"Our Master and our God bids that this be done."
